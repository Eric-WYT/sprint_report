SPRINT

Learning Athletic Humanoid Sprint Skills from Five Motion Sequences via Spectral Priors

Abstract

Generating human-like sprinting for humanoid robots remains a significant challenge, primarily due to the scarcity of diverse motion capture data and the instability of rapid gait transitions. In this work, we propose SPRINT, a system that learns athletic humanoid skills from only five motion sequences via spectral priors. Our approach centers on a frequency-adaptive Spectral Prior Generator. This is based on the insight that the inherent periodicity of human gait allows for the synthesis of motion priors across continuous velocity ranges, even from limited data fragments. By combining these priors with motion correction and composition modules, we develop a control policy that achieves a peak sprinting velocity of 6.0 m/s while maintaining natural motion styles. We further introduce a zero-shot sim-to-real transfer pipeline for the Unitree G1 humanoid platform. Physical experiments demonstrate that SPRINT enables seamless transitions from walking to sprinting, confirming that spectral priors provide a robust foundation for highly dynamic humanoid locomotion.
